Amazon: Photos Multi-Device

Hybrid design management/lead designer role for personal media services for the largest online retailer and cloud service provider in the world.

ContextWorking at corporate in-house at Amazon headquarters in Seattle
RoleProduct design, cross-platform design, design systems, design leadership

The context

Amazon is the largest online retailer and cloud service provider in the world. Amazon Photos is their photo storage, sharing, and printing service for consumers, available on desktop, tablet, mobile, web, television, and smart device platforms.

Browsing albums on the Amazon Photos app on the Echo Show. The Photos experience encompasses many platforms and touchpoints.

My role encompassed both design managerial responsibilities for a team of 15+ design employees, and lead design work for desktop/mobile web, iOS, Android, television, and smart devices. In the following org chart, you can see my managerial people scope.

On the righthand side, my direct reports are in dark blue, and additional team operations management for other UX employees are in light blue. Work credit: Leonard "Leo" Pak

The problem

When I first joined the Amazon Photos team, design employees were reporting directly to product managers. However, this arrangement wasn't scaling well as the design team was growing beyond 10 employees. Design inconsistencies, process gaps, knowledge silos, etc. were becoming increasingly common.

As a hybrid manager/individual contributor hire, how would I help resolve these team issues, while also delivering design work for multiple devices?

The management/design process

As a design manager, I sought to centralize design operations to reduce fragmentation. These changes could be summarized as:

  1. Centralizing team operations
  2. Centralizing project management
  3. Centralizing people management
  4. Establishing design principles and team culture
  5. Establishing best practices for design process/delivery
  6. Establishing better cross-functional collaboration
  7. Contributing to the greater Amazon design community

Centralizing team operations

As designers had been working in silos, I made changes for the design employees to operate more as a team. This ranged from logistical changes like newly created shared team email, chat rooms, wiki pages, tools, asset management, etc., to building habits to feel more like a team, like holding weekly design reviews and moving seating to the same area of the building.

The results (and some reflection)

During my tenure, I was able to establish centralized team processes/outputs and grow team headcount by roughly 50%.

Guest Center has continued to get more customers through fleshing out capabilities and working toward a self-service model (see the self-service signup wireframes excerpt below for another design example).

OpenTable was a good learning experience for me to better understand the challenges of transitioning from legacy softwares. During my time at OpenTable, I also took on some design team leadership responsibilities focused on team logistics and collaborating with a remote team. OpenTable was also one of my best experiences working collaboratively with product managers and engineers.
