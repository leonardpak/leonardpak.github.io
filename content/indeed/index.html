Indeed: Internationalization

Hybrid design management/lead design work for internationalization of the largest job search site in North America.

ContextWorking at corporate in-house in Tokyo, Japan offices
RoleProduct design, design localization, design management

The context

Indeed is the largest job search site in North America, and has been rapidly expanding internationally.

Initially, my role at Indeed encompassed both design managerial responsibilities and lead design work for desktop/mobile web, email, and chat services. My initial managerial people scope was as follows:

On the righthand side, my direct reports are in dark blue, and I also collaborated closely with other design employees in light blue. Work credit: Leonard "Leo" Pak

Eventually, as we entered a post-COVID working environment, I transitioned to a full-time lead designer role.

A humble disclaimer

As Indeed is my most recent in-house corporate work, many works are confidential and unable to be shared, due to not having been made public.

The management/design process

In my initial years, I focused primarily on management of a young design team.

Setting up better team foundations

To set up better foundations for the team, I focused on a few key areas in order to improve team operations:

  1. People management: running design reviews, handling performance management and hiring
  2. Research operations: utilization of and collaboration with research vendors, developing a quicker feedback cycle process
  3. Project management: managing a kanban board for design tasks, running planning/retrospectives and backlog management
  4. Collaboration/delivery: improving handoffs and design file management

These types of improvements were key to functioning well within Indeed's heavily process-driven, developer-centric culture.

Engaging within the design community

I also spent time engaging with the greater design community. This included hosting UX events in Tokyo, disseminating design education materials within Indeed Tokyo, creating better collaborative spaces in our offices, and helping hire for other teams in Tokyo.

Running collaborative design workshops

Indeed has a deeply rooted culture of doing collaborative exercises. Therefore, another of my key responsibilities was running collaborative design workshops. I frequently ran collaborative workshops both in-person and remotely.

Examples of artifacts from both in-person and remote collaborative design workshops. Above are images of empathy map exercises done with groups in-person. Below is an image of brainstorming sessions done with groups remotely.

The goals of these workshops were often to:

  1. Create clarity and alignment between functions on the problem, goals, and purpose
  2. Identifying potential issues, approaches, requirements, risks, and concepts
  3. Helping plan and prioritize coming quarters/years

Switching to full-time lead designer role

After COVID changed the way we worked, I switched to a full-time lead designer role. My works ranged from working on consumer/B2B web/chat products, to internal localization tools.

Design: creating job profiles via LINE and getting job recommendations

Many businesses in Japan engage with consumers using a popular chat app in Japan called LINE. Indeed wanted to leverage LINE to message prospective job seekers of new job possibilities.

Through LINE, users could receive images with interactive hotspots to facilitate interaction. In order to create designs for this, I not only created visual assets, but also provided code for use in both prototypes and production.

Examples of assets created, along with corresponding code handed off to developers. Above is an image used to invite LINE users to see job information, with code indicating interactive hotspots. Below are interactive LINE "flex message" cards being used to display job information, being simulated using production-capable code. Work credit: Leonard "Leo" Pak

Normally, I do not recommend jumping into code for designs, but with the specific constraints of the LINE messaging app, and a desire to create intended interactions for use in a prototype for usability testing, it made sense to pursue.

Consequently, with the help of a UX developer, I was able to create a working prototype within the LINE chat app, and then later created a usability research guide for our research vendor to use in usability testing.
